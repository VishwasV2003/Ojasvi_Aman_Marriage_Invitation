Celebrations

All gatherings take place at one venue—see location at the end of the page for the address and directions. Dress to celebrate in traditional Indian style where you can—we cannot wait to host you.

5 AM

Ganesh Sthapna

A sacred start—we seek Lord Ganesh Ji’s blessings for harmony and auspicious beginnings for all our festivities.

Theme: Serene dawn, marigold and ivory—peaceful, devotional tones.

Dress code: Traditional Indian attire; light pastels, white, or cream preferred.

8–9 AM

Chak Bhaat

A warm morning with loved ones—shared rituals and laughter over a traditional spread.

Theme: Homely warmth—fresh florals and earthy tones.

Dress code: Comfortable ethnic or smart-casual for a relaxed morning.

10:30–1:30

Haldi

A vibrant Haldi carnival—golden hues, music, and blessings before the big day.

Theme: Sunshine and joy—yellows, whites, and bursts of green.

Dress code: Yellow, white, or cream; avoid black. Flowy fabrics welcome.

3 PM – 5 PM

Sagai

Ring ceremony and celebration—a promise sealed with rings, full of cheer and tradition.

Theme: Festive romance—blush, gold accents, candlelight softness.

Dress code: Festive Indian or Indo-western; semi-formal welcome.

7 PM onwards

Ladies sangeet

Songs, dance, and sparkle—a vibrant night celebrating the women who light up our lives.

Theme: Bollywood glam—sequins, jewel tones, fairy lights.

Dress code: Festive lehengas, gowns, or shararas; statement jewellery encouraged.

6:15 PM

Baarat Swagat

Drums, colour, and joy—we welcome the baarat with open arms and dancing feet.

Theme: Royal procession—rich reds, gold, and festive marquee.

Dress code: Traditional Indian festive; comfortable footwear for dancing.

7:15 PM

Phere

Around the sacred fire we take our vows—your blessings mean the world to us.

Theme: Timeless mandap elegance—jasmine, deep greens, gold accents.

Dress code: Wedding-traditional; guests in formal Indian attire.

9 PM onwards

Reception

Dinner, toasts, and dancing—let’s celebrate late into the night.

Theme: Evening glamour—crystal lights and starlit celebration.

Dress code: Formal evening Indian or black-tie optional.

May your home be filled with laughter, your hearts with patience, and your days with countless small joys.
